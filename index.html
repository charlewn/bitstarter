Unlike any other animal, fish have a special sense that allows them to determine the speed and direction of currents, helps them hover in place and even swim upstream. Known as lateral line sensing, it also helps fish find the underwater “sweet spot” to find food that may be tumbling down the river. Around the world, underwater vehicles have been deployed for several decades to track pollution, inspect ships hulls for damage, and for surveillance. But their big drawback is limited battery life. If successful, this new device would allow vehicles to travel more efficiently through the water like a fish, saving time and battery life.

Underwater camera technology will allow fishermen or researchers of the sea to collect demographics of underwater life activities. Instead of hiring a crew of educated professionals and investing on aquatic equipments, an underwater camera fish will save millions of dollars to collect underwater data. With this technology, it will open many possibilities that cannot be done before.
